# About Me

Hi, I’m Ralph.

I’m a PhD student in Human-Centered Computing at Clemson University, where I focus on designing and evaluating **human-AI systems** powered by large language models (LLMs). My work sits at the intersection of **AI, human-computer interaction (HCI), and behavior**, with a particular interest in how AI systems shape how people think, learn, and make decisions.

## What I Work On

I build and study systems that integrate AI into real-world workflows. Currently, I contribute to **Quizzibility**, an AI-powered educational platform that combines:

- Interactive learning environments (quizzes, assignments, coding tasks)
- LLM-driven feedback and analysis
- Instructor-facing tools for understanding student behavior and misconceptions

Through this work, I explore questions like:
- How can AI systems support learning without encouraging over-reliance?
- What interaction designs lead to deeper understanding rather than shortcutting?
- How can we make AI outputs interpretable and actionable for users?

## My Research Direction

My broader goal is to design **human-AI interaction systems that improve human reasoning**, not replace it.

While my current work uses education as a domain, my interests extend to:
- AI copilots and assistants
- Developer tools and agentic systems
- Productivity and decision-support systems
- AI evaluation and trust calibration

## What I’m Building

I’m actively developing:
- LLM-powered feedback and analysis systems
- Interfaces for surfacing patterns in user behavior (e.g., misconceptions)
- Tools that combine AI with structured interaction design

I care deeply about building systems that are not only functional, but **thoughtfully designed around how people actually use them**.
